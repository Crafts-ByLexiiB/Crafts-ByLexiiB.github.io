Honey Butter Toast

Prep Time: 10 minutes Cook Time: 20 minutes
Total Time: 30 minutes Yields: 6

Ingredients

  • 6 or 12 slices white sandwich bread (use 12 if the bread is thin and stack them in pairs; use 6 if the slices are thick)
  • 1 cup unsalted butter, melted
  • 1/3 cup honey
  • 3/4 cup brown sugar
  • 1/2 tablespoon vanilla extract
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt

    Instructions

    1. Preheat the oven to 350°F.
    2. If you're using thin white sandwich bread, use 12 slices and stack two on top of each other to make 6 thicker pieces. Just make sure the bread isn't too soft—something like fresh Wonder Bread won't hold its shape well. If you're using a whole loaf, slice it into pieces about 1.75 to 2 inches thick.
    3. Slice off all the crusts before moving on.
    4. In a microwave-safe bowl, mix together the melted unsalted butter, honey, light brown sugar, vanilla, and a pinch of salt. Microwave for 30 seconds to 1 minute, then whisk really well until it turns into a smooth, glossy syrup.
    5. Coat each piece of bread generously on all sides with the syrup and place them on a nonstick baking pan—no parchment needed.
    6. Bake for 15 minutes, then carefully flip each one and bake for another 5 minutes until golden and sticky all over.
    7. Let them cool for about 10 minutes before serving. They’ll feel soft right out of the oven but will crisp up beautifully as they cool slightly. Enjoy them warm!

      Chili Oil Double Crunch

      Prep Time: 15 minutes Cook Time: 15 minutes
      Total Time: 30 minutes Yields:2 1/2 cups

      Ingredients

      • 2 1/2 cups vegetable oil
      • 4 garlic cloves, thinly sliced
      • 1 shallot, finely chopped
      • 1/2 cup walnuts
      • 1/2 cup red chili flakes
      • 1 teaspoon salt
      • 2 teaspoons granulated sugar
      • 1 teaspoon white pepper
      • 2 tablespoons paprika

        Instructions

        1. Preheat your oven to 350°F. Spread the walnuts out on a baking sheet and toast for about 8 minutes, or until golden and fragrant. Let them cool completely, then finely chop. Set aside for later.
        2. While the walnuts cool, get your heatproof glass jar ready. In the jar, add the red chili flakes, salt, sugar, white pepper, and paprika and stir together.
        3. In a small pot, heat the vegetable oil over medium heat to 350°F. Add the sliced garlic and fry until golden brown and c rispy, stirring often so it doesn’t burn. Use a slotted spoon to transfer the garlic to a paper towel to drain
        4. In the same oil, add the finely chopped shallots and fry until deeply golden and crispy. Remove with a slotted spoon or strain with a sieve over a heat proof bowl and set on a paper towel to drain.
        5. Once the garlic and shallots are done, strain the oil through a fine mesh sieve to catch any leftover bits. Return the strained oil to the pot and reheat it until shimmering and hot.
        6. Carefully pour the hot oil into the prepared jar over the spice mix. It’ll sizzle immediately — that’s exactly what you want!
        7. Once the chili oil has slightly cooled, about 5 minutes, stir in the reserved fried garlic, fried shallots, and chopped toasted walnuts.
        8. Let your chili oil cool completely at room temperature. Seal the jar with a lid and store in the fridge — it’ll last for weeks and just gets better over time.
